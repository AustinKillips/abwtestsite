Grit MTB Festival

FAQ

Common questions about getting to Grit Fest, riding, camping, food, bikes, and showing up for the first time.

Who is Grit Fest for?

Grit Fest is for trans women, trans men, cis women, and non-binary riders of all abilities and backgrounds.

Do I need mountain bike experience?

No. The First Touch clinic is designed for people who are MTB curious and ready to try off-road riding in a safe, supportive setting.

Is camping included?

Yes. Camping is included in festival tickets, with dispersed camping and nearby parking at Centennial Park.

Can I come without a mountain bike?

Yes. Demo bikes are available for riders who do not have access to a mountain bike, and local rentals can be listed here too.

Are meals included?

Meals are part of the weekend experience, along with morning coffee and tea, sparkling water, and refill stations.

What should I bring?

Bring your bike gear, camping setup, layers, rain gear, reusable mug and utensils, water bottle, and anything you need to feel comfortable outside for the weekend.
